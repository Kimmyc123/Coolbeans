    <div class="container"> 

        <h1 style="text-align: center; color: #003366; margin-bottom: 30px; border-bottom: 3px solid #CC0033; padding-bottom: 15px;"> 

            Cool Beans: Effects Of Increasing Pulse Consumption On Cardiovascular Disease Risk Factors 

        </h1> 

 

        <!-- Study Purpose --> 

        <div style="border: 3px solid #003366; padding: 20px; margin-bottom: 30px; border-radius: 8px;"> 

            <h2 style="color: #CC0033;">Study Purpose</h2> 

            <p>The purpose of this USDA funded trial is to assess the effects of consuming two doses of pulses (1.5 cups/wk and 3 cups/wk) on:</p> 

            <ul> 

                <li>CVD risk factors (blood lipids, blood pressure, and HbA1c)</li> 

                <li>Measure changes in overall diet quality</li> 

                <li>Determine if pulse consumption alters self-reported satisfaction with life and food in adults</li> 

            </ul>

        <!-- Public Health Context --> 

        <div style="border: 3px solid #CC0033; padding: 20px; margin-bottom: 30px; border-radius: 8px;"> 

            <h2 style="color: #003366;">Public Health Context</h2> 

            <ul> 

                <li>60% of U.S. adults live with at least one chronic disease, and 40% have two or more diet-related chronic conditions</li> 

                <li>Current dietary patterns contribute significantly to this health burden</li> 

            </ul>

        <!-- Pulses as a Nutritional Intervention --> 

        <div style="border: 3px solid #003366; padding: 20px; margin-bottom: 30px; border-radius: 8px;"> 

            <h2 style="color: #CC0033;">Pulses as a Nutritional Intervention</h2> 

            <ul> 

                <li>Pulses (beans, peas, lentils, chickpeas) are nutrient-dense foods featured in many healthy dietary patterns</li> 

                <li>Key nutritional characteristics: high fiber, low glycemic index, high protein, low fat, and rich in bioactive compounds</li> 

                <li>Current Dietary Guidelines for Americans (DGA) recommend 1.5 cup-equivalents of pulses per week</li> 

                <li>Evidence suggests increasing to 3 cup-equivalents weekly may provide additional health benefits</li> 

            </ul>

            div.style.display = 'none'; 

        }); 

        // Handle both numeric weeks and the "about" section 

        const targetId = isNaN(weekNum) ? 'week-' + weekNum : 'week-' + weekNum; 

        document.getElementById(targetId).style.display = 'block'; 

        links.forEach(function(link) { 

            link.classList.remove('active'); 

            if (link.getAttribute('data-week') == weekNum) { 

                link.classList.add('active'); 

            } 

        }); 

    } 

 

    // Add click event listeners to navigation links 

    links.forEach(function(link) { 

        link.addEventListener('click', function(e) { 

            e.preventDefault(); 

            showWeek(this.getAttribute('data-week')); 

        }); 

    }); 

 

    // Show week 1 by default on page load
